# Linear Algebra for Large Language Models

A technical guide exploring the mathematical foundations of modern AI. This book bridges the gap between abstract linear algebra and the practical architecture of Large Language Models (LLMs).

---

## About the Book
This project is designed for engineers and researchers who want a geometric and algebraic intuition of how data flows through a model. I am releasing this work in two distinct phases to ensure the mathematical foundations are peer-reviewed before moving into specific architectures.

### Part 1: Foundations (Available Now)
* **High-Dimensional Vector Spaces:** Understanding embeddings and latent space geometry.
* **Matrix Decompositions:** The role of **SVD** and Eigenvectors in model compression.
* **Vector Operations:** The mechanics of projection and similarity in AI.
* **Attention Mechanisms:** The linear algebra behind Scaled Dot-Product Attention.

### Part 2: Applications (in progress)
* **Neural Networks as Linear Transformations:** Mapping input spaces to output spaces through weight matrices and non-linearities.
* **Transformer Architecture:** A deep dive into the algebraic structure of encoders, decoders, and residual connections.
* **Tensors & Transformations:** Multi-dimensional operations across model layers.
* **Reasoning via Latent Space:** How models "think" through vector navigation and high-dimensional semantic relationships.
* **Optimization Geometry:** Understanding loss landscapes through an algebraic lens.
* **Mamba, Samba:** State space models
